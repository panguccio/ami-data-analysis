# ami-data-analysis

**Acute Myocardial Infarction (MI)** (“heart attack”) is caused by the formation  of a clot in one of the coronary arteries that supply blood to the heart muscle. Mortality is substantial in the period immediately after the event (and during the years after surviving the initial infarction). 

The final aim of this project is to predict the probability of 30-day mortality following an AMI. 

Therefore, the objective of this first part of the analysis is to explore the characteristics of the patients included in the study and compare subjects who were alive at 30 days with those who died within 30 days.
